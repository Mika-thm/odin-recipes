pumpkin soup

Description

This is a very simple dish that is very reminiscent of the fall and winter. It is warm and comforting. It is also very calming to make. Using a whiole squash for this dish is a great expereince ot remind oneslf of the season. Something about cleaving a large squash open on the kitchen counter feels very involved. Also scooping out the seeds feels very much like autumn!

Simmered kabocha is a hearty dish that is great as a side to many other foods. It is calming and easy to digest. It only requires a couple of ingredients

it is really a dish that is about waiting and simplicity

ingredients

  • Kabocha squash
  • Water
  • Soy sauce
  • Brown sugar
  • dashi
  • mirin
  • like much of the cooking I do, measure to taste

Steps

  1. Wash off the outside of the kabocha squash with cool water
  2. use a cleaver to cut the squash in half (be careful)
  3. scoop out the seeds from the halves. (They can be saved for roasting)
  4. place the kabocha halves face down so they are stable on the cutting board
  5. cut the halves into equally sized wedges
  6. cut the wedges into equally sized cubes/pieces
  7. add enough dashi, water, soy sauce, mirin into a shallow pot with a drop lid
  8. add the kabocha pieces in one layer in the pot, facing up
  9. close the lid and bring turn the heat to low
  10. allow the kabocha to simmer in the liquid
  11. after the kabocha simmers in the lquid for about 10 minutes, open the lid and sprink brown sugar over the pieces
  12. close life and continue to simmer until the pieces are tender enough to easily pierce with a toothpick. This time will depend on the size of your pieces
  13. remove kabocha from pot and serve
